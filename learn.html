Knowledge is Power. Action is Change.

Learn simple, effective ways to reduce your environmental impact and live more sustainably.

üí° Reduce Electricity Use

Your home's energy consumption is a major part of your carbon footprint. Small changes here make a big difference.

  • Switch to LEDs: LED bulbs use up to 85% less electricity than incandescent bulbs and last much longer.
  • Unplug Devices: Many electronics use "phantom power" even when off. Unplug chargers and appliances when not in use.
  • Use Fans, Not Just AC: In moderate weather, a ceiling or pedestal fan can be just as effective as an air conditioner, using a fraction of the energy.
  • Optimize Fridge Temperature: Keep your refrigerator set between 2¬∞C and 3¬∞C and your freezer at -18¬∞C for maximum efficiency.

üíß Conserve Water

Saving water not only preserves a precious resource but also saves the energy used to pump, treat, and heat it.

  • Fix Leaks Promptly: A single dripping tap can waste thousands of litres of water per year.
  • Take Shorter Showers: Aim for a 5-minute shower. This can save a significant amount of water and heating energy.
  • Reuse Water: Use leftover water from boiling vegetables to water your plants (once it's cool).
  • Run Full Loads: Only run your washing machine and dishwasher when they are completely full.

ü•¶ Reduce Food Waste

When food is wasted, all the energy and resources used to produce it are wasted too. Decomposing food in landfills also releases methane, a potent greenhouse gas.

  • Plan Your Meals: Buy only what you need. A weekly meal plan and a shopping list are your best tools against impulse buys.
  • Store Food Correctly: Learn the best ways to store fruits and vegetables to prolong their freshness.
  • Embrace Leftovers: Get creative with leftover meals for lunch the next day to ensure nothing goes to waste.
  • Compost: If you can, compost your food scraps. This enriches soil and avoids methane emissions from landfills.
